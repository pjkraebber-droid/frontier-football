### 📋 TOP STORY

**Oakland Roots leave the Coliseum.** This is the headline that matters most for USL Championship's long-term map. 
After two seasons at the Oakland Coliseum, Oakland Roots SC announced it will not return to the stadium for the 2027 season.
 
The club cited limitations around event control, matchday flexibility, fan experience, and operating costs as the reasons for leaving.
 The stakes here are real: 
in March 2025, the Coliseum set a United Soccer League attendance record for a home opener when 26,000 fans showed up.
 Now that crowd is a memory. 
The club has not secured an alternative venue for the 2027 season yet, but said it is exploring short-term options like a pop-up venue in the Coliseum's parking lot and transforming its training facility in Alameda into a matchday venue.
 For the longer term, 
the club will continue to pursue its interest in building a permanent home at the Howard Terminal site, with meetings underway to secure an Exclusive Negotiating Agreement with the Port of Oakland.
 
Seven more home games remain at the Coliseum between July 25 and October 10.
 Make of that what you will — but a club playing a farewell lap at a landmark stadium, without a confirmed successor, is a genuinely open situation.
