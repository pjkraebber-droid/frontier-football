### 📌 FRONTIER NOTE — USL Premier / Pro/Rel Watch

**North Carolina FC's dormancy is a live case study in what USL Premier entry actually costs.**


On November 4, 2025, the club announced it was suspending operations pending an application to join the future USL Premier in 2028.
 
NCFC chairman Steve Malik has been attempting to build a soccer-specific stadium in the Raleigh area since 2019. Despite some regulatory approvals and acquiring the needed land, Malik hasn't been able to secure the needed public financing for the stadium project to move forward.
 
The USL believes Raleigh has strong potential as a future Division One market if it meets the league's professional standards, including a minimum 15,000-seat, purpose-built soccer stadium that will serve as an anchor for real estate development.


That gap — between "we've applied" and "we've broken ground" — is where clubs go dormant and supporters wait. North Carolina FC's story isn't unique; it's a preview of the pressure every USL Premier hopeful faces. 
The club had unveiled plans for a 22,000-seat soccer-specific stadium in downtown Raleigh that could expand to 28,000, but that project has faced funding hurdles for years.
 The 2028 clock is real. So is the financing gap.
