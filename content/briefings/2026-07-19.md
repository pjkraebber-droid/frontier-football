### 📋 TOP STORY

**Trevor Amann Writes USL Championship History on National TV**


Trevor Amann made USL Championship history with the fastest hat trick ever in the league — three goals only 3 minutes and 27 seconds apart — as the Pittsburgh Riverhounds came away with a 3-1 win over Louisville City FC at F.N.B. Stadium.
 That's the headline, and it deserves full context: this wasn't a scrimmage blowout. 
The battle between the reigning league champion Hounds and the reigning Players' Shield holders Louisville began like a playoff match, with both teams feeling their way into the game.
 
Jansen Wilson scored from the penalty spot to end the Hounds' shutout bid in a match aired nationally on CBS, marking Pittsburgh's first network TV appearance since winning the USL Championship Final last November.


Context matters on two levels. First, the historical weight: 
the win was the first for the Hounds at home against Louisville in an all-time series largely dominated by road teams, and it completed Pittsburgh's first season sweep of Louisville.
 Second, the standings picture: 
Tampa Bay leads the Eastern Conference with 31 points from 15 games; Charleston Battery sit second on 26; Detroit City are third on 24
 — meaning Pittsburgh, though surging, still has serious ground to make up if they want home-field in October.
