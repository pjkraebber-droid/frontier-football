### 📌 FRONTIER NOTE — USL Premier / North Carolina FC


On November 4, 2025, North Carolina FC announced it was suspending operations and would not field a team in 2026, to focus on launching a USL Premier team by 2028.
 
The club has formally filed an application to join the USL's proposed Division One league, stating the move "begins a formal process that could bring top-level professional soccer to Raleigh."
 
The USL's position: Raleigh has potential as a future Division One market contingent on a minimum 15,000-seat, purpose-built soccer stadium.
 
Chairman Steve Malik has been pursuing a shared stadium with the NWSL's North Carolina Courage since 2019 but has been unable to secure the needed public financing to move forward.
 This is the central unresolved tension in the NCFC story — the application is filed, the vision is clear, but the stadium financing is the actual gating factor. Until that piece moves, 2028 remains aspirational.
