## 📋 TOP STORY

**Pittsburgh vs. Louisville on CBS — The League's Biggest Stage, At the Worst Possible Time for the Hounds**


Week 20 of the USL Championship season features the return of the league to the CBS Television Network, as defending titleholders Pittsburgh Riverhounds SC play host to Players' Shield holder Louisville City FC at F.N.B. Stadium.
 The noon kickoff is a genuine showcase moment for Tier 2 soccer — a national broadcast audience, two of the Eastern Conference's marquee clubs, and genuine stakes for both sides.

The problem for Pittsburgh is context. 
The Riverhounds (6-6-2, 20 points) sit seventh in the conference, on the heels of back-to-back losses to expansion sides Brooklyn FC and Sporting JAX. They were also eliminated in the group stage of the USL Cup.
 
The most recent loss — a 2-1 defeat to Sporting Jacksonville Wednesday — was particularly damaging because Jacksonville entered that match winless on the season.
 
Pittsburgh has been solid defensively, allowing only 17 goals — only six other USL teams have conceded fewer — but offensively the Hounds have scored just 16, with 18 other clubs having netted more.


Louisville brings genuine form. 
Forward Jansen Wilson is in the best season of his career, with a team-high 10 goals and four assists in all competitions, including five goals and two assists in league play, and was named to the USL Cup Team of the Week after scoring twice in last week's win over Lexington SC.
 
Louisville has never lost in Pittsburgh — a 7-0-5 all-time record at FNB that stands as a USL Championship record unbeaten run for a visiting opponent.
 
Pittsburgh does get Albert Dikwa back from suspension, and both Guillaume Vacter and Sam Bassett have recently returned from injury.

## 📌 FRONTIER NOTE

**USL Premier (T1, launching 2028) — Scholes Era Begins Now**


Tony Scholes — formerly Chief Football Officer of the English Premier League — begins his role as USL Premier president in Summer 2026
, which means his tenure has effectively started. 
USL Premier will operate as a single national table targeting 20 clubs, scheduled to launch in 2028; the Championship will also consolidate to a single national table of 20 clubs; and the USL will introduce America's first promotion and relegation system, creating a connected merit-based professional pathway.


The stadium piece is moving in tandem. 
Wilton Rancheria and Sacramento Republic FC have scrapped their phased-expansion plan and committed to building a 20,000+ seat venue at the Railyards from day one, adding 8,000 seats over the original proposal.
 
Construction will continue through 2027, with the stadium expected to be completed in 2028.
 Republic's move is a direct USL Premier positioning play.

Separately: 
North Carolina FC dropped out of the USL Championship following the 2025 season and went on hiatus, with hopes of entering USL Premier in 2028 with a new stadium in Raleigh.
 
The new Division One league requires clubs to have a stadium with a minimum capacity of 15,000 seats
 — a bar that is actively reshaping the ambition landscape for every serious club in the pyramid.
