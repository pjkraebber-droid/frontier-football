## 📌 FRONTIER NOTE — USL Premier (2028)


The USL announced earlier this year that its new Division One league will be called "USL Premier," with a targeted launch date of 2028. It will sit atop a structure that includes the pre-existing USL Championship and USL League One.



USL President Paul McDonough has indicated he expects eight USL Championship clubs to move up immediately, with the inaugural league likely carrying 12–14 franchises, with plans for two-up, two-down promotion/relegation from the start.



Pittsburgh Riverhounds, Sacramento Republic, Detroit City, and Miami FC are all currently in the process of building new stadiums
 with USL Premier entry in mind. 
Louisville City FC has formally applied to join USL Premier at launch; applications are under review and inaugural members will be announced at a later date.



USL Premier will signal the launch of promotion and relegation across all three USL tiers — the first time pro/rel will operate between professional leagues in the United States.
 For clubs currently in League One watching the Championship, and for League Two clubs building toward full professionalism, this structural shift is the most consequential development in American soccer's lower tiers in years. Every club's infrastructure investment, attendance trajectory, and ownership depth is now being read against a 2028 deadline.
