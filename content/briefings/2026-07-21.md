## 📋 TOP STORY

**Trevor Amann writes himself into USL Championship history — on national television.**


Pittsburgh's Trevor Amann needed just three minutes and 27 seconds to score a hat trick — the shortest such span in USL Championship history — leading the Pittsburgh Riverhounds to a 3-1 win over Louisville City FC on Saturday in a nationally televised game on CBS.
 The timing matters. 
Amann's hat trick came in the opening five minutes of the second half, with goals in the 47th, 48th, and 50th minutes.
 
Louisville's Jansen Wilson pulled a goal back from the penalty spot in the 68th minute
, but the damage was done.


For Pittsburgh, it was the club's first home win over Louisville in a series going back 12 seasons, and it ended a six-game unbeaten run for LouCity across all competitions.
 The table implications are real: 
LouCity (7-6-3, 24 points) remains fourth in the Eastern Conference — seven points back of Tampa Bay for the league lead, and five points clear of eighth-place Hartford Athletic for the final playoff spot.


For LouCity's interim bench, the postgame was blunt. 
"We talked about at halftime, the first five minutes of the second half is crucial, and we let ourselves down massively," said interim head coach Simon Bird. "And we can't be responding to adversity the way we did."
 
The boys in purple finished with more shots than Pittsburgh (12-10), more possession (55%-45%), and higher expected goals (1.51-1.18)
 — a stat line that will sting precisely because the game was there to take.

**North Carolina FC | USL Premier 2028 Application — Status Check**

This story is now eight months old but remains structurally significant for the pyramid. 
North Carolina FC's franchise agreement with the USL Championship concluded at the end of the 2025 season. The club has formally applied to join USL's new Division One league launching in 2028.
 The bottleneck has always been the stadium: 
the USL has stated that Raleigh has "strong potential as a future Division One market if it meets the league's professional standards, including a minimum 15,000-seat, purpose-built soccer stadium."
 
Despite some regulatory approvals and acquiring the needed land, owner Steve Malik hasn't been able to secure the public financing for the stadium project to move forward.
 The club is dormant, the application is pending, and the stadium remains unbuilt. No material update as of July 21, 2026 — but the clock is running. 2028 is not far away.
