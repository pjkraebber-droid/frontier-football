### 📌 FRONTIER NOTE — North Carolina FC's USL Premier bet remains stuck on stadium financing

This is context, not news, but it's the most structurally significant story in the American soccer pyramid right now. 
North Carolina FC's franchise agreement with the USL Championship concluded at the end of the 2025 season; the club will not compete in the Championship in 2026 and has formally applied to join USL's new Division One league launching in 2028.
 The sticking point: 
the USL requires a minimum 15,000-seat, purpose-built soccer stadium.
 
Owner Steve Malik has long been in pursuit of a new stadium, even pitching Kane Realty's Downtown South property — but the Downtown South project has not gotten off the ground, and financing for the stadium remains unclear.
 
The decision to essentially go dormant for two seasons comes at an awkward time for the USL, which is also attempting to implement a system of promotion/relegation.
 Until Raleigh secures a financing path, North Carolina FC's USL Premier application is aspirational, not structural.
